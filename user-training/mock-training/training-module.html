Introduction

🎣 What is Phishing?

Phishing is the most common form of cyberattack. Criminals send convincing fake emails that appear to come from trusted sources — Microsoft, your bank, your employer, Canada Post — to trick you into revealing passwords, clicking malicious links, or downloading malware.

📊 The numbers: Over 90% of data breaches begin with a phishing email. In 2023, the average cost of a phishing breach exceeded $4.9 million CAD.

Phishing bypasses firewalls, antivirus, and email filters by targeting the human element. An attacker only needs one person to click once to gain a foothold inside an organization.

Why This Training Matters

You are the last line of defence. Technical controls stop the majority of attacks — but the attacks that get through will land in your inbox. A few seconds of caution before every click can stop nearly every phishing attack cold.

✅ After this training you will be able to identify phishing emails, avoid credential theft, and report suspicious messages correctly.

⚠️ The 8 Warning Signs of Phishing

Memorise these red flags. Check for them before clicking anything in any email.

🚨

1. Urgency & Threats

"Act NOW or lose access." Pressure tactics stop you from thinking clearly. Legitimate services don't demand instant action under threat.

📧

2. Mismatched Sender

The display name says "Microsoft" but the actual email address is from a random domain. Always click the sender name to reveal the real address.

🔗

3. Suspicious Links

Hover before you click. The URL must match the real company domain. Beware lookalike domains (microsoft-secure.xyz) and URL shorteners.

📎

4. Unexpected Attachments

.exe, .zip, .js, .lnk files in emails you didn't expect are almost always malicious. When in doubt, call the sender to verify.

🔑

5. Requests for Credentials

No legitimate organization will ever ask for your password by email. Ever. Full stop.

👤

6. Generic Greetings

"Dear Customer" instead of your name means the sender likely doesn't have a real account record for you — classic bulk phishing.

💰

7. Unusual Requests

CEO wire transfers, HR requests for your SIN, IT requests for your password — always verify unusual requests through a separate channel before acting.

🔒

8. HTTPS ≠ Safe

The padlock icon only means your connection is encrypted. It does NOT mean the site is legitimate. Phishing sites use HTTPS freely.

Quick check before every click: Does the sender domain match? Does the link go where it claims? Was I expecting this? If uncertain — don't click. Verify first.

📞 Reporting Procedure — Remember This

If you receive a real or suspected phishing email:

  • Forward it to soc@company.local
  • Use the Report Phishing button in your email client
  • Call IT Security: ext. 4357
  • Slack: #report-phishing

There is NEVER a penalty for reporting — even false alarms are valuable signals.
