# Security Awareness Metrics Analysis
**Enterprise Phishing Simulation & Automated Defense**
**Measurement Period:** Q1 2025 (January — March)

---

## Executive Summary

The phishing awareness program delivered significant, measurable improvements across all tracked metrics over the 8-week program period. The click rate fell from 70% to 10% (an 85.7% reduction), credential submission dropped to zero, and the reporting rate increased by 500%. These results demonstrate the direct ROI of structured security awareness training combined with realistic simulated phishing campaigns.

## Topics That Showed the Most Improvement

**1. Credential Submission Awareness (+100% improvement)**
The most critical metric — no users submitted credentials in Round 3 compared to 4 in Round 1. The training module's emphasis on "no legitimate system asks for your password by email" was the most impactful single lesson.

**2. Reporting Behaviour (+500% improvement)**
Perhaps the most important cultural shift: users went from passive non-reporters to active reporters. Six of ten users reported the Round 3 phishing email before anyone clicked, which in a real scenario would have enabled near-instant organizational containment.

**3. Link Inspection (click rate reduction)**
Training on the "hover before you click" technique and suspicious TLD recognition drove the click rate from 70% to 10%. The single click in Round 3 came from a user who had scored only 60% on the post-training quiz.

## Recommendations for Ongoing Program

**1. Maintain quarterly simulation cadence**
Running simulations every 3 months keeps awareness high without causing simulation fatigue. Annual simulations alone are insufficient — research shows awareness retention drops significantly after 6 months.

**2. Diversify templates each round**
Use different attack themes per campaign. Round 1 (MFA update), Round 2 (password expiry), Round 3 (benefits enrollment) produced meaningfully different results per template, validating the need for theme diversity.

**3. Prioritize reporting rate as the #1 KPI**
Click rate is important, but the reporting rate is the metric that matters most for organizational resilience. A user who reports a phishing email protects all colleagues — a user who simply doesn't click helps only themselves. Target: 70%+ reporting rate by end of Q2.

**4. Recognize and reward reporters publicly**
Users who reported the simulation in Round 3 should be acknowledged (with their permission) in the company newsletter or a Slack announcement. Positive reinforcement accelerates cultural change.

**5. Track per-department vulnerability**
Break down metrics by department in future rounds. In this cohort, Finance and Operations showed higher click/submission rates than IT and Legal — departments may benefit from role-specific training content.

**6. Integrate training completion into onboarding**
New employees are statistically the most vulnerable to phishing in their first 90 days. Require completion of this training module and a simulated phishing test within the first 2 weeks of employment.
